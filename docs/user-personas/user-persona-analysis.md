| Role                     | Professional Interior Designer / Sales Consultant      |
| Age                      | 26–40                                                   |

| Technical Expertise      | Low – Not a software power user; uses simple tools     |

| Primary Device           | Laptop                                                  |
| Sharing Method           | Export as PDF/Image to email clients                   |

## Background Story

Thushara works at a mid-range furniture showroom, meeting 5–8 clients daily. She designs living rooms, dining rooms, bedrooms, office spaces, and business stores. She is **not a software power user** (1/5 in 3D design) but values quick, professional presentations. She finds that 3D models rarely reflect real lighting or external conditions, which frustrates both her and her clients.

## Goals

- Enter exact room dimensions quickly using numerical input  
- Drag templates to modify irregular wall shapes  
- Apply colors to individual furniture items  
- Recover mistakes via Undo and confirmation pop-ups  
- See a loading bar during model generation  
- Work in a clean, clutter-free interface  
- Export final design as PDF or image for client sharing  

## Frustrations / Pain Points

- Slow loading times  
- Cluttered interface or too many buttons  
- 3D models not reflecting real-world lighting or furniture placement  
- Fear of losing work if mistakes are made  

## Key Quote

> “It’s very difficult to show exactly how furniture will look in 3D compared to real placement, especially with lighting and window direction.”

# 👤 Persona — Customer: Mohammed Farsi

## Profile Overview

| Attribute                | Details                                                 |
|--------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------|
| Name                     | Mohammed Farsi                                         |
| Role                     | Homeowner / Individual Customer                        |
| Age                      | 41–55                                                   |
| Tech Experience          | 0–2 years with furniture/design software              |
| Software Proficiency     | 1/5 (complete beginner)                                |
| Technical Expertise      | Low – Limited experience with digital tools            |
| Speed Priority           | 5/5 (wants things to work without delay)               |
| Primary Device           | Desktop PC / Mac                                       |
| Sharing Method           | Viewer link to review the 3D model from home           |

## Background Story

Mohammed is renovating his home and wants to furnish a living room, dining room, and bedroom. He walked into a furniture store expecting guidance from a consultant, but when the salesperson opened a 3D tool, he immediately felt lost. He is not tech-savvy and feels self-conscious about appearing confused in front of others. He either needs a very simple interface he can glance at comfortably, or he prefers to watch the designer control the tool while giving feedback.

## Goals

- Select pre-set room templates without manual configuration  
- See realistic shading to visualize the room  
- Receive confirmation before deleting anything  
- See a loading animation with time estimate  
- Read measurements in large, clear font  
- Receive a shareable viewer link to review the design at home  
- Have the salesperson control the tool while he observes  

## Frustrations / Pain Points

- Feels lost when using complex 3D tools  
- Fear of making mistakes or breaking the design  
- Small text and unclear measurements  
- Overwhelmed by too many buttons or confusing interface  

## Key Quote

> “Technical Difficulty: I'm not a tech person — 3D tools usually feel too complicated, and I'm afraid I'll get stuck or frustrated.”
